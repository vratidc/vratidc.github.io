overview: Khat-Phat is a VR stealth experience inspired by a childhood memory of retrieving a cricket ball from a neighbor’s backyard without waking up a sleeping uncle. Set in a culturally rooted rural Indian environment, the project explores how ordinary domestic sounds become amplified through a child’s fear of being caught. Using sound as the primary gameplay mechanic, players navigate through obstacles, manage a dynamic sound meter, and experience tension built from micro-interactions rather than explicit threats. The project blends nostalgia, social anxiety, spatial perception, and time pressure to create an immersive, emotionally grounded stealth experience. </br></br>
